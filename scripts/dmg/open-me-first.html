Opening Horizon for the first time

A quick one-time step, then you'll never see it again.

Horizon is free and open-source. macOS shows an “Apple could not verify” warning because the app isn’t notarized. That needs a paid Apple Developer account, which this free app doesn’t use. The app is safe to open.
  1. Drag Horizon onto the Applications folder (if you haven’t already).
  2. Open your Applications folder and double-click Horizon. macOS will say it “could not verify” the app. Click Done.
  3. Click the button below to jump to Privacy & Security. Scroll down to the Security section, click Open Anyway next to Horizon, then confirm with Touch ID or your password.
    Open Privacy & Security →

    Button didn’t do anything? Open System Settings → Privacy & Security manually and scroll to the bottom.

That’s it. Horizon opens and lives quietly in your menu bar (top-right). You only do this once.

Comfortable with Terminal? Skip the clicks.

Paste this and press Return, then open Horizon normally:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Horizon.app
